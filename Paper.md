# PTK Superionic Composite  
### Phytic Acid – Triflic Acid – Keratin (PTK)

A bio-derived, theoretically conceived superionic composite designed for high-efficiency proton exchange membranes (PEMs).

---

## 📌 Overview

The **PTK Superionic Composite** is a conceptual material that integrates:

- Phytic Acid (high-density phosphate network)  
- Triflic Acid (superacid proton source)  
- Keratin (bio-derived functional scaffold)  

This system is designed to overcome the **"dead weight problem"** found in conventional PEMs like Nafion, where most of the material does not contribute to proton conduction.

---

## ⚡ Key Idea

> Replace inert structural mass with **fully functional, ionically active components**

---

## 🧪 Core Components

### 1. Phytic Acid (IP6)
- Natural molecule from agricultural waste
- Contains **6 phosphate groups**
- Functions:
  - Proton donor network
  - Crosslinker
  - Water retention agent
  - Thermal stabilizer

---

### 2. Triflic Acid (CF₃SO₃H)
- One of the strongest known superacids
- Provides:
  - High proton concentration
  - Mobile triflate ions
- Creates:
  - Hydrophobic microdomains
  - Channelized proton pathways

---

### 3. Keratin
- Extracted from biological waste (hair, wool, feathers)
- Unlike synthetic polymers:
  - Contains **active functional groups**
  - Participates directly in proton conduction
- Reduces inactive mass significantly

---

## ⚙️ Mechanism of Conductivity

The PTK system enables proton transport through:

- **Grotthuss mechanism** (proton hopping)
- **Vehicular transport** (ion movement)
- **Hydrophilic channel conduction**

---

## 🔗 Synergistic Stability

- Triflic acid → boosts proton availability  
- Phytic acid → stabilizes + buffers system  
- Keratin → provides structural + conductive framework  

➡️ Result: **Stable superionic composite instead of degradation**
